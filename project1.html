Nature for Wellbeing

We will be running our project in collaboration with Bristol Future Parks across two locations. Our project's aim is to benefit the local community by facilitating connection to the natural world through workshops and events and to motivate reciprocal work which benefits people and their environment.

The workshops will run in 6 week rotations and will be based on the 5 ways to Wellbeing and the 5 ways to Nature Connectedness. They include mindfulness activities, practical rural skills, conservation and nature crafts.

We will focus our work primarily in supporting adults (aged 18+) facing mental health challenges and other interest groups who come from challenging backgrounds or face barriers to connection

Our project will be working closely with communities to connect them to local green spaces and find ways of engaging in them so as to enable people to participate in the support and improvement of their local environment, creating a sense of purpose and place.
